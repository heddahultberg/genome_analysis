# Independent Project in Genome Analysis (1MB462)
_Niphotrichum japonicum_ is a moss with high tolerance to extreme environments, such as drought and intense heat. By studying this plant one can hopefully gain more insight into what makes a plant heat resilient, and therefore serves as an excellent model. This project will explore the heat-stress response mechanisms of _Niphotrichum japonicum_, with genome assembly, annotation and differential gene expression analysis. The project will take inspiration from the study presented by Zhou et al. (2023) in the article _"Chromosome-level genome assembly of Niphotrichum japonicum provides new insights into heat stress responses in mosses"_. 

In this project, I will: 
* Work with sequencing data (Illumina, Nanopore, Hi-C) on UPPMAX
* Inspect, process and analyze reads
* Investigate genome structure and gene content
* Identify heat-responsive genes
* Summarize and biologically interpret the results

This repository will serve as documentation of my workflow, code, results, and final reflections.
